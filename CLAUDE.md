## Project Overview

A satirical horror novella (or novel) narrated by a mid-career demon in hell's Hauntings department. The premise: horror movie tropes are a demon's actual job, and the job makes no sense from the inside. First-person, dry humor, deadpan. The horror set pieces are genuinely well-crafted; the comedy comes from the demon's backstage perspective.

Inspired by the observation that horror movie demons put enormous theatrical effort into scaring people when they could presumably just... drag them to hell. The book asks: what's the internal monologue? What's the motivation? Is this a chore?

**Current Status:** First draft complete (2026-04-11). 23 chapters, ~13,600 words. KDP-ready pipeline (PDF, EPUB, paperback, covers).

## Key Constraints

- **The horror must be real.** The demon is good at their job. Scare set pieces should be genuinely effective horror writing. If the horror doesn't work, the comedy of deconstructing it doesn't land.
- **The humor must be dry.** The demon is sincere. They don't know they're funny. No winking, no mugging, no fourth-wall breaks.
- **The demon is competent.** Not a bumbler. A skilled professional in an absurd system. The comedy is situational, not slapstick.
