# Consistency Auditor Report

**Date**: 2026-04-09
**Manuscript**: Demons at Work (13 chapters, ~9,574 words)
**Scope**: Full manuscript against all canonical lore

## Methodology

Cross-referenced all factual claims, spatial details, character states, timeline events, and worldbuilding elements in the manuscript against the canonical lore documents (themes.md, world.md, style.md, characters.md, outline.md) and against internal manuscript consistency.

## Findings

### HIGH

*No HIGH consistency issues found.*

### MEDIUM

#### M1. Grab bar height inconsistency

- **Location**: Ch1 "On Paper" line 67 vs Ch3 "The Canvas" line 29
- **Ch1**: "a grab bar installed on the left-hand wall at about waist height"
- **Ch3**: "The grab bar is on the left wall at hip height"
- **Problem**: "Waist height" and "hip height" are different positions. This is a physical detail described by the same observer (Tert) about the same object, first from a photograph and then in person. The discrepancy is small but noticeable to attentive readers.
- **Suggestion**: Harmonize to one term. "Hip height" is more accurate for ADA grab bars (typically 33-36 inches). Consider whether Tert's in-person observation corrects his photo estimate, which could be preserved as a deliberate detail (Tert refining his read of the space).
- **Confidence**: High

#### M2. Basement entrance: lore vs manuscript

- **Location**: Ch1 "On Paper" line 49 vs world.md
- **Lore (world.md)**: "A basement with its own separate staircase and a door that does not quite hang right"
- **Manuscript (Ch1)**: "a basement with an exterior entrance"
- **Problem**: The lore describes an interior staircase with a poorly hung door; the manuscript describes an exterior entrance. These are architecturally different features that affect how Tert accesses the basement during work shifts (Ch4, Ch6).
- **Suggestion**: Update the lore to match the manuscript, since "exterior entrance" is the operational detail that appears in the working chapters. Or if the poorly hung door matters thematically, revise the manuscript to include it.
- **Confidence**: High

#### M3. Recliner vs armchairs

- **Location**: world.md vs Ch3 "The Canvas"
- **Lore (world.md)**: "A recliner in the living room positioned for a view the spouse liked"
- **Manuscript**: Uses two armchairs throughout, one moved, one with a deep imprint. No recliner appears.
- **Problem**: The armchairs serve the same thematic function (one seat occupied, one abandoned) and work better dramatically (facing configuration, the imprint). But the lore specifies a recliner.
- **Suggestion**: Update lore to match manuscript. The armchair pair is superior for the scene in Ch3 where Tert sits in the wife's chair.
- **Confidence**: High

#### M4. Meri's arc incomplete against lore specification

- **Location**: Across manuscript; characters.md and themes.md
- **Lore (characters.md)**: Meri's arc is "questions -> evaluating answers -> making statements." Themes.md resolved: "Meri progresses from collecting data (writing in margins) to evaluating answers (stops writing) to making a statement (final scene)."
- **Manuscript**: Ch2 (questions, writes in margins), Ch5 (brief hallway exchange), Ch8 (stops writing: "They do not write this one down"), Ch11 (noticed by absence only). No statement scene exists.
- **Problem**: The "writing -> not writing" progression is beautifully executed. But the third stage (making a statement) is absent. The lore marks this as a resolved question, meaning it is canonical.
- **Suggestion**: This is a manuscript-incompleteness issue, not a contradiction. The missing post-turn chapters (see structure auditor) are where this scene would go.
- **Confidence**: High

### LOW

#### L1. "Subject" vs "Gordon" naming transition

- **Location**: Ch6 "Six Minutes Early" line 22
- **Text**: "I have started thinking of him as Gordon, which the Operations Manual advises against."
- **Observation**: Before Ch6, the manuscript uses "the subject" and "he." After Ch6, it uses "Gordon." This transition is clean and deliberate. The one irregularity: Ch7 does not use "the subject" at all, but Ch8 uses "my subject" (referring to Gordon in conversation with Meri). This is consistent: Tert uses "my subject" when speaking professionally, "Gordon" internally.
- **Status**: Not a problem. Noting for completeness.
- **Confidence**: High

#### L2. Timeline placement of mid-cycle review

- **Location**: Ch13 "Mid-Cycle"
- **Text**: Prim reviews "weeks one through four"
- **Observation**: A "mid-cycle" review of a five-week cycle should occur around week 2.5-3. Reviewing weeks 1-4 makes it a late-cycle review. This is likely intentional bureaucratic humor (the "mid-cycle" label is aspirational, not accurate), but could read as a timeline error.
- **Suggestion**: Consider one line of Tert's internal narration acknowledging the naming discrepancy, if it is intentional. If it is not intentional, the review should cover weeks 1-2 or 1-3.
- **Confidence**: Medium (could be intentional)

## Summary

The manuscript is remarkably clean on factual consistency. No contradictions rise to HIGH severity. The grab bar height and basement entrance are minor physical-detail divergences easily fixed. The recliner-to-armchairs change is an improvement the lore should absorb. The most significant finding is M4 (Meri's arc), which is an incompleteness issue tied to the manuscript's unfinished state.
