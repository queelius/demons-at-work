\chapter{The Report}

The shift report takes four minutes to write. It has always taken four minutes. The form has not changed. The fields are the same fields: date, case number, shift duration, activities performed, environmental adjustments, subject behavioral observations, Phase assessment.

I write: \emph{Subject exhibited new behavioral pattern: vocalized reading (solo, approx. 40 min). Source text unidentified. No escalation response required. Ambient conditioning maintained per schedule. No contact.}

This is accurate. Everything in the report happened. Nothing in the report did not happen. The report contains all of the relevant data and none of what the data was like.

I file it. I get coffee. It is the wrong temperature. I drink it anyway.

Meri is not in the break room. I notice this, which is new. I do not usually track who is and is not in a room I have entered for coffee.

On the counter next to the sink, someone has put a potted plant. The plant is dead. It has been dead for some time. Someone is watering it anyway. I have seen the water stains.

I go back to my desk. The window behind it shows the same thing it showed yesterday, which is unusual in its own way. I do not look at it closely.

I sit at my desk for a while.
