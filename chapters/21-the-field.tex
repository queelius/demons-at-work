\chapter{The Field}

The direct-interface report cannot stay blank.

I have left it for two days, which is longer than I have ever left a shift report unfiled. Prim has not asked about it. This is either because they have not noticed, which is unlikely, or because the Post-it was their way of giving me room, which I am using up.

I sit at my desk and I open the form.

\emph{Direct-perception event executed per Phase 4 protocol. Subject achieved visual contact. Duration: approximately three seconds.}

This is true. The three seconds happened. The thirty-seven minutes before them are not a required field.

\emph{Subject response: verbal engagement. Subject assessed operative as unauthorized entrant. No spiritual-framework indicators.}

``Verbal engagement'' is a category on the form. It is meant to cover whispered prayers, panicked phone calls to clergy, and the specific involuntary vocalizations that Apparitions logs as ``fear events.'' It was not designed to cover a retired English teacher asking a demon if he is all right.

I check the box. I file the form.
