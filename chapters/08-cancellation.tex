\chapter{Cancellation}

On Wednesday, Gordon's phone rings.

I am in the hallway. He is in the living room. He answers it. I can hear both sides. A woman's voice, warm, familiar. She is asking if he still wants to come to dinner on Saturday. She says the word ``everyone'' in a way that means a small group of people who know him and have been checking on him since his wife died.

``I think I will sit this one out,'' Gordon says. ``I have not been sleeping well.''

This is true. He has not been sleeping well because of me.

The woman on the phone says she understands. She says he should call if he changes his mind. She says they miss him. He says thank you. He says he will call. He hangs up.

He stands in the living room for a moment holding the phone. Then he puts it down and goes to the kitchen to make dinner for one.

I make a note in my margin pad: \emph{subject declining social contact; Phase 2 isolation indicators present.}
